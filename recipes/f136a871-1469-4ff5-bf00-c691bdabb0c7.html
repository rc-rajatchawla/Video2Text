Homemade Butter

This recipe guides you through the process of making fresh, creamy butter from heavy cream using a food processor. You'll learn how to churn the cream into butter, separate the buttermilk, and finish the butter for immediate use or storage. Optionally, you can add salt or other flavorings to customize your butter to your liking.

Step 1

Begin with 2 cups of heavy cream, preferably at room temperature, to ensure a smoother churning process.

Step 2

Pour the heavy cream into the bowl of your food processor, making sure the lid is secure.

Step 3

Start the food processor on a medium speed and let it run for approximately 2-3 minutes, until the cream thickens and reaches the whipped cream stage.

Step 4

Continue processing past the whipped cream stage. You'll notice the cream starting to clump together and yellow slightly; this is the butter beginning to form.

Step 5

Keep the processor running for another 3-5 minutes. Watch for the moment when the butter solids separate from the liquid, which is the buttermilk. This usually happens with a noticeable change in sound as the mixture becomes less viscous.

Step 6

Once separated, turn off the processor and use a spatula to gather the butter together. Remove the butter from the processor and place it onto a clean surface or bowl.

Step 7

Press the butter with a spatula or wooden spoon to expel as much buttermilk as possible. You can also use cheesecloth or a clean kitchen towel to squeeze out the liquid.

Step 8

If desired, mix in a pinch of salt to the butter for flavor. This is also the time to add any other flavorings, such as herbs or garlic, if you choose.

Step 9

Shape the butter into a block or roll, then wrap it in parchment paper or place it in an airtight container.

Step 10

Refrigerate the butter until firm, or use it immediately. The leftover buttermilk can be stored in the refrigerator and used for baking or cooking.

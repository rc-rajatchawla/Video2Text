Garlic Herb Anchovy Butter

This garlic herb anchovy butter combines the umami-rich flavor of anchovies with the aromatic freshness of herbs and the pungent kick of garlic. It's perfect for spreading on crusty bread, melting over grilled steak, or tossing with hot pasta.

Step 1

Begin with half a cup of softened unsalted butter, ensuring it's pliable enough to mix with other ingredients.

Step 2

Incorporate two tablespoons of finely minced fresh herbs, such as a combination of parsley, chives, and thyme, into the butter for a fragrant touch.

Step 3

Mash in two teaspoons of anchovy paste or finely chopped anchovies, adjusting the amount to suit your taste for a savory depth.

Step 4

Add one to two minced garlic cloves, depending on how garlicky you prefer your butter.

Step 5

Squeeze in about half a teaspoon of fresh lemon juice to add a subtle brightness to the butter.

Step 6

Season the mixture with a pinch of salt, if needed, and freshly ground black pepper to taste, considering the natural saltiness of the anchovies.

Step 7

Mix all the ingredients together until they are thoroughly combined and the butter has a uniform appearance.

Step 8

Transfer the butter mixture onto a piece of parchment paper or plastic wrap and shape it into a log or other desired form.

Step 9

Chill the wrapped butter in the refrigerator until firm, which will make it easier to slice. It can be stored in the refrigerator for up to two weeks or frozen for longer storage.

Step 10

When ready to use, slice off the desired amount from the chilled butter log. It can be used directly from the fridge or freezer.

Anchovy Butter

Anchovy butter is a rich and savory compound butter that combines the umami flavor of anchovies with the freshness of parsley and a hint of acidity from lemon juice. It's perfect for adding depth to grilled meats, roasted vegetables, or simply spreading on a crusty piece of bread. This recipe will guide you through the steps to create a flavorful anchovy butter that can be stored and used to enhance a variety of dishes.

Step 1

Begin by allowing half a stick of unsalted butter to soften at room temperature until it is easily malleable.

Step 2

In a mixing bowl, combine the softened butter with a generous tablespoon of anchovy paste, ensuring the paste is evenly distributed throughout the butter.

Step 3

Finely chop fresh parsley leaves to yield one to two tablespoons and fold them into the butter mixture, adding a fresh herbal note.

Step 4

Squeeze in about a teaspoon of fresh lemon juice to the mixture, providing a bright and tangy flavor that will cut through the richness of the butter.

Step 5

Season the butter with a small pinch of salt, being cautious with the quantity as anchovy paste already contains salt.

Step 6

Crack fresh black pepper over the mixture to taste, adding a slight heat and aromatic quality.

Step 7

Stir all the ingredients together until they are fully incorporated and the butter has a uniform consistency.

Step 8

Lay out a piece of cling film or parchment paper and place the mixed butter in the center. Shape it into a log or your preferred shape by rolling it within the wrap.

Step 9

Refrigerate the wrapped anchovy butter until firm, which will make it easier to slice. It can be stored in the refrigerator for up to a week or frozen for longer storage. When needed, simply cut off slices from the chilled or frozen butter to use in your dishes.

Sabudana Khichdi

Sabudana Khichdi is a popular Indian dish made with tapioca pearls, peanuts, and a mix of spices. It's a common food during fasting days but is also enjoyed as a delicious breakfast or snack. The key to a good khichdi is to get the sabudana pearls to a soft yet non-sticky consistency, and the use of spices and aromatics to bring out the flavors.

Step 1

Start by rinsing 1 cup of sabudana (tapioca pearls) under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the sabudana in water for at least 4-6 hours or overnight. The water level should be about 1-2 inches above the sabudana. This step is crucial for achieving the right texture.

Step 2

After soaking, drain the sabudana thoroughly to remove excess water. Check if the sabudana pearls are soft by pressing one between your fingers. It should crush easily.

Step 3

Dry roast 1/2 cup of raw peanuts in a pan until they are crunchy and their skins start to crack. Allow them to cool, then rub them between your hands to remove the skins and coarsely grind them in a mortar and pestle or a food processor.

Step 4

Heat 2 tablespoons of ghee (clarified butter) or oil in a non-stick pan or wok. Add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds and let them sizzle until they start to crackle.

Step 5

Add 1 finely chopped green chili and 1/2 cup of diced potatoes to the pan. Saut the potatoes until they are golden and cooked through.

Step 6

To the pan, add the drained sabudana, ground peanuts, 1 teaspoon of salt (or to taste), and 1/2 teaspoon of sugar. Mix gently to combine all the ingredients without breaking the sabudana pearls.

Step 7

Cover the pan with a lid and let the khichdi cook on a low flame for about 5-7 minutes. The sabudana pearls should become translucent, indicating that they are cooked.

Step 8

Uncover the pan and add freshly chopped coriander leaves and 1 tablespoon of lemon juice for freshness. Gently fluff and mix the khichdi.

Step 9

Serve the Sabudana Khichdi hot, garnished with additional coriander leaves and accompanied by yogurt or raita on the side.
